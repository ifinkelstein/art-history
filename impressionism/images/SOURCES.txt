IMAGE SOURCES & LICENSES
========================

All seven paintings are in the PUBLIC DOMAIN (the artists died well over 95 years
ago). The scans are hosted on Wikimedia Commons. The download scripts pull them
through Wikimedia's official Special:FilePath endpoint, which returns a JPEG at a
chosen width. If you ever need to fetch one by hand, open the Commons page and
click "Original file" (or "Download").

Each entry: local filename  <-  Commons file page  |  approx. original resolution

REAL OBJECT / HISTORICAL PHOTOS (these replaced the old hand-drawn diagrams)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
The presentation no longer contains any drawings or SVGs — every visual is a real
photograph. The 1915 advertisement and the 1715 self-portrait are public domain.
The mineral and palette photos are freely licensed on Commons (typically CC-BY or
CC-BY-SA) — open the file page and read the author / licence box before any PUBLIC
redistribution. For private classroom display you are fine.

old-palette-selfportrait.jpg
  Self-portrait of Joseph Vivien with a palette, c. 1715 — stands in for the old
  "mud" palette of dull earth colours used before the 19th century. PUBLIC DOMAIN.
  https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Self-portrait_of_Joseph_Vivien_with_Palette,_ca._1715.jpg

lapis-lazuli.jpg
  Raw lapis lazuli — the gemstone natural ultramarine blue was ground from.
  https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Lump_of_raw_lapis_lazuli.jpg

artist-palette.jpg
  A real artist's palette loaded with bright paint.
  https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Paintbrush_and_palette.JPG

oil-colors-ad-1915.jpg
  1915 magazine advertisement for F. W. Devoe & C. T. Raynolds Co. artists' oil
  colours (incl. Cadmium Yellow), from The International Studio. PUBLIC DOMAIN.
  Commons file (note the curly quotes in the exact name):
  https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:%E2%80%9CF._W._Devoe_%26_C._T._Raynolds_Co.%E2%80%9D_%E2%80%9CArtists%E2%80%99_Oil_Colors%E2%80%9D_%E2%80%9CCADMIUM_YELLOW%E2%80%9D_September_1915_ad_-_The_International_studio_(IA_internationalst5622unse_0)_(page_14_crop).jpg
  If the downloader can't fetch this one (its filename is fiddly), open
  Category:Oil paint on Commons and grab any period oil-colour advert by hand.

Note: the earlier pig's-bladder and dot-demo diagrams were removed. The pig's
bladder is now mentioned in the lesson text instead, and Seurat's real La Grande
Jatte already shows the pointillist dots when you zoom in.
